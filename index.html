Airline Hubs Webapp

Airline pilots have many options when deciding which airlines to fly for. The impending pilot shortage has airlines struggling to get pilots to choose their company over competing airlines. Due to the nature of the industry it is not advantageous to switch between airlines mid-career, so a pilot is usually stuck working for the same airline until they retire. The only variety they have is, as a pilot gains seniority in an airline, the opportunity to choose which hub to be based out of. Since there is no visual of each airline's hubs, a pilot cannot easily rank which airline is best for them based off location. I am planning on pulling together the hubs for each major airline, large cargo airline, and larger regional airline and map them all in one place. My initial thought is to use CartoDB because I can store the information from across the internet in a database and easily visualize in CartoDB. There is no consolidated information available so the data gathering will be the biggest part of this project.

South Philly Open Data

The main idea for this project is to use Philadelphia’s open data to create a trash bin desert map in South Philadelphia. While recently walking through South Philly, I noticed that there was an alarming amount of trash in the streets. There were also almost no trash cans to be found. South Philly will continue to collect trash if the city does not provide receptacles to dispose of litter. By providing a trash desert map, there will be a visualization of blocks that are in the most need of trash bins. The city of Philadelphia’s open data portal has the data for the location of their waste wire trash bins as well as the Big Belly trash bins.
